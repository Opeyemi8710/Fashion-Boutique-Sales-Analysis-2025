# Retail Fashion Boutique Analysis (2025)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive data-driven analysis of a fashion boutique’s performance throughout 2025. By analyzing a dataset of 2,176 transactions, the study evaluates the relationships between brand performance, pricing strategies, and customer satisfaction to drive future growth and operational efficiency.

## 📖 Executive Summary
## Introduction
This report presents a detailed evaluation of retail performance, identifying key revenue drivers and systemic inefficiencies. The analysis leverages Excel-based statistical modeling to provide actionable business intelligence.

## The Problem
The boutique faced challenges in identifying true profit drivers and understanding the impact of its discounting strategy. Without empirical evidence, it was unclear if markdowns were driving volume or simply diminishing profit margins and brand value.

## The Process
The analysis followed a rigorous four-stage workflow:

**Data Cleaning:** Standardized dates and resolved inconsistencies within the transaction records.

**Descriptive Analysis:** Built Pivot Tables to aggregate total revenue, top-performing brands, and seasonal trends.

**Diagnostic Analysis:** Utilized Pivot Charts and Slicers to visualize return reasons and inventory health.

**Statistical Testing:** Performed Correlation and Multiple Linear Regression analysis to measure the impact of markdowns on Estimated Revenue.

## The Solution & Interpretation
The analysis identified Outerwear as the primary revenue leader and August as the peak sales period.

### Key Statistical Finding:

Correlation analysis revealed a weak negative relationship (-0.239) between markdown percentage and revenue. This indicates that higher discounts do not significantly drive sales and may actually slightly reduce overall revenue.

With an R-Square of only 0.057, the regression model confirms that price markdowns explain very little of the boutique's success, suggesting customers are prioritized by brand reputation and seasonal necessity over deep discounts.

## Final Recommendations
Strategic Inventory Shift: Increase stock levels for high-demand Zara products and the high-satisfaction Shoes category.

Pricing Policy Reform: Move away from broad, store-wide discounts. Focus markdowns only on slow-moving "Accessories" or "Tops" as identified in the inventory risk analysis.

August Marketing Focus: Allocate the majority of the annual marketing budget toward the August peak to capitalize on proven seasonal demand.

Return Mitigation: Improve quality descriptions and size guides for Outerwear to reduce the "Changed mind" return rate.
